The Global Stroke Burden

What is a Stroke?

A stroke occurs when blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes.

There are two main types: ischemic (caused by clots) and hemorrhagic (caused by bleeding). Smoking increases risk for both types.

Key Stroke Statistics:

  • 15 million people suffer stroke worldwide each year
  • 5 million die annually from stroke
  • Smokers have 2-4 times higher stroke risk
  • Every 40 seconds, someone in the US has a stroke

The FAST Warning Signs

Recognizing stroke symptoms quickly can save lives. Tobacco use doubles your stroke risk.

F

Face Drooping

Does one side of the face droop or feel numb?

A

Arm Weakness

Is one arm weak or numb? Ask them to raise both arms.

S

Speech Difficulty

Is speech slurred or hard to understand?

T

Time to Call

If any symptoms appear, call emergency services immediately.

Prevention Strategies

1

Primordial Prevention

Discouraging smoking initiation by educating about stroke risks:

  • Paralysis: One side of the body may become permanently weak or immobile
  • Aphasia: Loss of ability to understand or express speech
  • Cognitive impairment: Memory loss and difficulty thinking
2

Primary Prevention

Educating the public on FAST to reduce stroke incidence through:

  • Smoking cessation: Quitting reduces stroke risk by 50% within 1 year
  • Early recognition: FAST signs help identify strokes immediately
  • Risk factor control: Managing blood pressure, diabetes, etc.
3

Community Empowerment

Training non-medical individuals to act as first responders:

  • Bystander training: Teaching FAST recognition in workplaces/schools
  • Emergency protocols: Simple steps to take when stroke is suspected
  • Cultural adaptation: Tailoring messages for different communities
4

Cost-Effectiveness

Utilizing existing tobacco packaging regulations:

  • No new infrastructure: Uses existing cigarette packaging
  • Minimal costs: Only design/printing changes needed
  • High visibility: Each smoker sees warnings 20+ times/day

Stroke Clock Algorithm

Help estimate stroke onset time with these critical questions

1. When was the patient last seen normal?

2. Can the patient form new memories? (assess anterograde amnesia)

3. Are symptoms fluctuating or fixed?

4. Is there facial droop in old photos or videos?
